BTS HISTORY

BTS: Global K-pop Icons

BTS is a South Korean K-pop band that became an international sensation in the late 2010s. Formed by Big Hit Entertainment, the seven members—Jin, Suga, J-Hope, RM, Jimin, V, and Jungkook—are known for their singing, rapping, and dancing. Their fanbase, called ARMY, has grown to millions, thanks to BTS’s strong social media presence and numerous record-breaking hits, such as "Dynamite" and "Butter."

Rise to Fame

BTS debuted with their first EP, 2 Cool 4 Skool, in 2013. Their popularity skyrocketed as they released albums like Wings (2016) and embarked on their first world tour. Their success continued with Love Yourself: Tear (2018), which topped the Billboard 200 chart. BTS is renowned for addressing social issues in their music, often focusing on themes like self-love and empowerment.

Breaking Records

BTS has set numerous YouTube records, including most views in 24 hours for their music videos "Boy with Luv" (2019), "Dynamite" (2020), and "Butter" (2021). They've also topped international charts and made history as the first K-pop group to perform on shows like Saturday Night Live. Global Recognition

The group won the Billboard Music Award for Top Social Artist every year from 2017 to 2021, beating top artists like Justin Bieber. BTS was also nominated for Grammy Awards and performed at the ceremony in 2021. Their popularity extended to Japan with several Japanese albums, and their English singles, "Dynamite" and "Butter," dominated global charts.

Philanthropy & Leadership

BTS partnered with UNICEF in 2017 for the Love Myself campaign to support youth against violence. In 2021, they were appointed special envoys by South Korea's president and delivered a speech at the United Nations, advocating for vaccination and positive change during the COVID-19 pandemic. In 2022, they met with U.S. President Joe Biden to discuss rising violence against Asian Americans.
